Flexible Joint Webexperiment: Model derivation

Can you derive the mathematical model describing the flexible joint experiment?

Components

The Rotatary Flexible Joint experiment can be used to investigate resonance phenomena and vibrations. This labatory experiment consits of 4 parts,

  1. the base,
  2. the head,
  3. two springs, and
  4. the arm.

The parts are illustrated in the figure below:

The head is attached to the base, and can be rotated along the \(z\) axis via a motor (not shown in the figure). The rotation of the head relative to the \(x\) axis is denoted by \(\varphi_M\). Attached to the base is the arm, which can rotate freely along the \(z\) axis. Let the arm angle relative to the \(x\) axis be denoted \(\varphi_A\), while the angle relative to the head be denoted \(\alpha\). As shown in the figure, springs are attached to the head and the arm via screws, creating a connection between the arm and the head.

Goal

The goal of this experiment is to control the arm angle \(\varphi_A\) by manipulating the input voltage \(V_M\) of the motor.


Modeling

In the following subsection the equations needed for deriving the overal system model are given.
Springs

The torque generated by the sprigns is approximated by a linear torsion spring with a stiffness \( K_s \).

Following this, the potential energy of the springs can be found via \[V = \frac{1}{2} K_s \alpha^2.\]
